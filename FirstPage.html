In today’s age of globalization, people are moving more than ever. It is estimated that in 2015, there were 244 million migrants worldwide, which constitutes 3.3% of the global population (McAuliffe & Ruhs, 2017). These migration trends call for substantial research to understand the processes that individuals, societies and the global arena are undergoing in response to these changes.

Inter-Cultural Awareness Workshops

Learn about cultural competence and the ways in which you can be better prepared for todays globalized world.

For ethnocultural groups to integrate, multiple multicultural pre-conditions must prevail. These pre-conditions include ‘multicultural ideology’, or the appreciation, value and acceptance of cultural diversity. Relatively low levels of prejudice, and positive mutual attitudes should appear within and between social groups. In addition, all groups should feel a sense of attachment to, or identification with, the larger society (Kalin & Berry, 1996). Berry (2011) adds to these pre-conditions the necessity of equitable participation and equal access to institutions and cultural groups in the larger society.

What do these words even mean?

Multiculturalism
The presence of, or support for the presence of, several distinct cultural or ethnic groups within a society.
Globalization
The process by which businesses or other organizations develop international influence or start operating on an international scale.
Cross-Cultural Competence
The knowledge, skills, and affect/motivation that enable individuals to adapt effectively in cross-cultural environments.
